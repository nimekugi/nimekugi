Each nihontō is a silent witness of history.

We believe these swords deserve more than a display case.
They deserve to be known: their origins traced, their craftsmanship celebrated, their journey through the ages respected.

To own a nihontō is to become the guardian of this legacy.

二
The name Nimekugi (貫) refers to the bamboo peg that secures the handle of a Japanese sword — a small but essential part of its structure.
It reminds us that even the humblest details matter, a quiet force that holds strong symbols of bond and transmission.
